Crypto-Benchmark Analysis

Symmetric Ciphers (AES & DES) use the same secret key for both locking and unlocking. They operate on data blocks directly using quick bitwise operations (XORs, bit shifts, substitution tables). This makes them incredibly fast and suitable for bulk encryption of large payloads, files, or images.

Asymmetric Ciphers (RSA) use modular exponentiation over large prime numbers. The mathematical operations are significantly heavier and require much larger key sizes (e.g. 2048 bits) to offer similar protection. Because of this speed penalty and strict payload size limits (~190 bytes), RSA is never used for encrypting bulk data; instead, it is only used for key exchanges (encrypting symmetric keys) or digital signatures.
